The Real Problem

Your machine
was not built
for this.

A standard home washer can hold 12–15 lbs. A queen comforter weighs 7–10 lbs dry — but when wet, it doubles. The machine never fully agitates it, rinses it properly, or spins it dry. It just wraps around the drum and stays there.

Problem 1

It doesn't fit, and you know it.

Stuffing it in forces one side to clean while the rest stays compressed, damp and untouched.

Problem 2

Dust mites stay. Every night.

A half-washed comforter still holds millions of dust mites, skin cells and bacteria. The smell fades. The biology doesn't.

Problem 3

Hours of work. Still not clean.

  • ✕ Takes over your washer for hours
  • ✕ Dryer can't fully dry the fill — stays damp inside
  • ✕ Clumping and uneven fill after each wash
  • ✕ Risk of mold growing inside the filling
