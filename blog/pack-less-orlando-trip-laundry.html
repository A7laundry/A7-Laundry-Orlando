Orlando trips are notorious for overpacking — the parks, the pool, the Florida heat and a week's worth of "just in case" outfits. But hauling a giant suitcase (and paying to check it) is avoidable. The trick pros use: pack a light capsule and plan a single laundry pickup mid-trip.

Why Everyone Overpacks for Orlando

It's the combination: hot, humid park days mean you sweat through clothes; pool afternoons add swimwear and towels; and kids go through outfits fast. So people pack for every day of the trip — and end up lugging (and re-checking) far more than they need.

The Pack-Less Strategy: Half the Clothes + One Pickup

Instead of packing 7 days of clothes for a 7-day trip, pack 3–4 days' worth and plan one laundry pickup around the midpoint. You re-wear everything clean, your suitcase is half the size, and you skip a checked bag. One laundry pickup and delivery in Orlando resets your whole wardrobe.

A Light Orlando Capsule (Per Person)

  • 3–4 shirts/tops (quick-dry are great for the heat)
  • 2–3 shorts or bottoms
  • 1–2 swimsuits
  • Underwear & socks for 4–5 days
  • 1 light layer for cool restaurants/evenings
  • Comfortable park shoes + sandals

Add a small laundry bag to keep dirty clothes together for pickup.

When to Schedule Your Mid-Trip Pickup

Around day 3–4 of a week-long trip is ideal — you send off the first half's clothes and get them back clean for the rest. Families can even set up more than one pickup. If you're traveling with kids, our family vacation laundry guide has more.
