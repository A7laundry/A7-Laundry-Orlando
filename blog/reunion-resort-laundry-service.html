The Laundry Scale Problem at Reunion Resort

A standard 6-bedroom Reunion Resort vacation home generates a significant laundry load at each turnover. Let's walk through what that looks like:

  • 6 bedrooms × 2 beds each = 12 beds, each needing sheets + 2–4 pillowcases
  • 12–16 bath towels per bathroom (some properties have 6–8 bathrooms)
  • Hand towels, washcloths, kitchen towels, and dishcloths
  • Pool towels — large properties often stock 20–30 pool towels per stay
  • Decorative pillow covers, throw blankets, and soft goods throughout the home

Total: a typical 6-bedroom Reunion Resort turnover generates 35–50 lbs of laundry. For an 8–10 bedroom property, that can reach 60–80 lbs.

Here's the capacity problem: even a property with two full-size washers and dryers can only handle 1–2 loads simultaneously. A full 40-lb laundry load takes 3–4 machine cycles running back-to-back — meaning 6–8 hours of in-unit laundry time. On a property with a 10:00am checkout and 4:00pm checkin, that leaves virtually no margin for anything to go wrong.

Professional pickup handles everything in one trip. We take the entire load at once, wash at commercial scale, fold, and deliver back — in time for the next guests.
