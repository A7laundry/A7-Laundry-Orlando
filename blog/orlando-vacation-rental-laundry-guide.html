Part 1

For Vacation Guests

If you're visiting Orlando for a week or longer — especially in a vacation home, condo, or VRBO — laundry becomes a practical issue. You're far from home, you've packed for a theme park trip, and by day 4, the reality of dirty pool clothes, sweaty T-shirts, and damp towels is very real.

Does Your Vacation Rental Have a Washer and Dryer?

Most vacation homes in Orlando — particularly in resort communities like Reunion Resort, Windsor Hills, and Champions Gate — include a washer and dryer. Condos and smaller rentals may or may not. Check your listing before you pack light and discover there's no in-unit laundry.

Even with in-unit laundry, using the machines during your stay takes time you may not want to spend. Between park days, pool time, and dinner reservations, laundry often doesn't happen until the last night — and then there's the frantic "we have an early flight" problem.

Your Options as a Guest in Orlando

Guests visiting Orlando have three practical laundry options:

  • In-unit washer/dryer: Available in most larger vacation homes. Works fine for a small load but slow for a family of 6 after a week of park visits.

Part 2

For Airbnb & VRBO Hosts

Managing laundry for an Orlando vacation rental is one of the most time-sensitive operational tasks in short-term rental hosting. Unlike most cities, Orlando vacation properties skew large (4–14 bedrooms), turnover quickly (high occupancy year-round), and guests arrive with extremely high expectations — largely because Disney-area accommodations have trained families to expect resort-quality linens.

Linen Inventory: How Many Sets Do You Need?

The standard recommendation for vacation rental hosts is 3 sets per bed: one on the bed, one in the laundry, one as a backup. This buffer allows for same-day turnovers without waiting for the laundry cycle to complete.

For a 4-bedroom property, that means stocking:

  • ~24 sets of sheets (8 beds × 3 sets = 24, accounting for different sizes)
  • ~30–40 bath towels across all bathrooms
  • 12–16 pool towels (if you have a pool)
  • 4–6 sets of kitchen linens

Washing Frequency by Item

Not every item needs washing at every turnover. Here's a practical schedule:

  • Sheets & pillowcases: Every guest stay, no exceptions
  • Bath & pool towels: Every guest stay
  • Comforters/duvet inserts: Every 3–4 stays (or every 8–12 if duvet covers are always used)
  • Mattress protectors: Monthly, or after any pet or illness stay
  • Decorative pillow covers: Every 2–4 guests
  • Throw blankets: Every 2–3 guests

Turnover Workflow: How Professional Hosts Handle It

High-performing Orlando hosts delegate laundry to a professional pickup service and structure their cleaning workflow around it:

  • Guest checkout: Cleaner strips all beds and bags all linens in provided XL bags
  • Laundry pickup: A7 Laundry picks up from the property — cleaner doesn't need to be present for handoff
  • Cleaning continues: Cleaner cleans the property while laundry is out
  • Laundry delivery: Clean, folded linens delivered back — within 24h standard, 6h on Express
  • Beds remade: Cleaner makes the beds with the delivered linens and the property is ready

This workflow eliminates the bottleneck of in-unit washing and dramatically reduces the cleaner's on-site time.
