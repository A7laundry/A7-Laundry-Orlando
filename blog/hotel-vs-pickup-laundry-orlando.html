Hotel In-House Laundry — Pros & Cons

Where hotel laundry wins

  • It's right there — drop it with the front desk or valet.
  • Great for a single item: pressing one shirt or suit for a meeting.
  • No coordinating with an outside service.

Where it falls short

  • Per-item pricing — a few dollars per shirt, more for pants; a normal vacation pile gets expensive quickly.
  • Built for valet, not volume — it's designed to press a few garments, not wash and fold a family's worth of clothes.
  • Hotel's schedule — turnaround depends on when the hotel runs it, not when you need it.

Pickup & Delivery Laundry — Pros & Cons

Where pickup wins

  • Per-pound pricing — $2.90/lb (Normal) handles big piles without per-item charges.
  • Free pickup & delivery — to and from your hotel; no car, no laundromat.
  • Full wash & fold — returned folded and ready to pack.
  • Same-day option — Express 6h, depending on availability.

Things to know

  • There's a $50 minimum order (free pickup & delivery included).
  • You arrange a pickup time — usually a quick WhatsApp message.
  • Same-day availability may vary, so ask for today's availability if you're in a hurry.

The Cost Difference: Per Item vs Per Pound

Here's the math that usually decides it. A typical vacation laundry bag is around 15–20 lbs. At $2.90/lb, that's roughly $50–$58 with free pickup and delivery. Billed per item at a hotel — say 25–35 garments at a few dollars each — the same load can easily run well over $100, depending on the hotel. For a single shirt, the hotel is fine. For a full pile, pickup is usually the clear winner.

Which Should You Choose?

  • Choose hotel laundry if you need one or two items pressed quickly and don't mind per-item pricing.
  • Choose pickup & delivery if you have a real pile — a family, a group, a long stay — and want it washed, folded, and delivered for a flat per-pound rate.
