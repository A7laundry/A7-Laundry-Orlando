Laundry Service Near Disney World: Best Options for Guests in 2026

April 11, 2026·5 min read·By A7 Laundry

You're three days into a week-long Disney trip. The kids' park shirts are soaked, your swimwear smells like a chlorine factory, and checkout is in four days. What do you do with the laundry?

This is one of the most common — and least-prepared-for — challenges that guests face near Disney World. Here's an honest breakdown of every option available to you, from worst to best.

Option 1: Your Vacation Rental's In-Unit Washer

Some vacation rentals near Disney include a washer and dryer. If yours does — great. But in practice, this means:

  • Running multiple loads over several hours
  • Someone has to stay back to move clothes
  • You're using your vacation time doing laundry
  • Many Disney-area homes use small, older machines

It works, but it's not a vacation activity. Most families end up doing laundry at 10pm or early morning — cutting into sleep and park time.

Option 2: Coin Laundromat Near Disney

There are laundromats off US-192 and Highway 27 in the Kissimmee/Davenport area. Expect to spend 2–3 hours and $8–$15 in quarters per load. The machines are often overloaded with other tourists. Parking can be tricky. And you're spending your park day doing laundry.

This is the most common choice for budget travelers — and the most time-consuming one.

Option 3: Disney Resort Laundry Rooms

If you're staying at an official Disney World resort, most offer coin-op or card-operated laundry rooms. Machines typically cost $3–$4 per wash/dry cycle. The rooms are basic, often shared, and you'll need to go back and forth to your room.

For families at off-site vacation rentals — this isn't an option at all.
