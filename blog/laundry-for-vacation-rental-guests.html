No Washer in Your Airbnb? Here's What Orlando Vacation Guests Do

April 12, 2026·5 min read·By A7 Laundry

You planned everything. The flights, the park tickets, the dining reservations. You packed just enough to keep the bags manageable. But somewhere around day three at Disney World, you realize you have a problem: the Airbnb listing said "laundry facilities available" — and what it meant was a coin-op machine three blocks down the street.

You're not alone. It's one of the most common surprises Orlando vacation guests run into, and it's worth knowing your options before you're down to your last clean shirt on a Wednesday.

The Problem Nobody Plans For

Most people booking vacation rentals in Orlando focus on what matters: proximity to the parks, pool access, the number of beds. Laundry is an afterthought — until it isn't.

A family of four spending a week near Disney World generates a lot of laundry. Think about it: daily park clothes, swimsuits, towels, kids' outfits that somehow absorb an ice cream cone every afternoon. By midweek, you're either doing laundry or buying new clothes at Disney Springs.

The listing said "washer/dryer" — but it was for a shared unit two buildings away. Or the machine is there but broken. Or the rental description was technically accurate when it said "laundry access nearby." These aren't lies, exactly. They're just not what you pictured.

Add in the fact that you're vacation-mode tired, you have a 7 AM park reservation tomorrow, and the last thing you want to do is spend three hours at a laundromat — and you've got a genuinely frustrating situation.

What Most Guests Do (And Why It Costs Them)

When the vacation rental doesn't have a working washer, guests typically fall into one of three patterns. None of them are great.

Option 1: The Coin Laundromat

There are coin laundromats near most vacation corridors in Orlando — along US-192 in Kissimmee, near International Drive, in Davenport. They work. The machines are usually functional. But the math is brutal when you're on vacation:

  • Drive time: 15–25 minutes each way from most vacation rental areas
  • Wait time: machines are often occupied, especially evenings and weekends
  • Wash + dry cycle: 1.5–2 hours minimum
  • You're stuck there — or driving back and forth
  • Total time cost: 3+ hours of your vacation, gone

That's an entire afternoon at the pool. Or a full evening at Disney Springs. People don't think about time as the cost, but when you're on vacation, it's the most expensive currency you have.

Option 2: Asking the Property Manager

Some guests message their Airbnb host or property manager hoping for a solution. Sometimes this works — occasionally a manager can point you to a specific machine or facility. More often, you get a polite response that confirms what you already suspected: there's no easy answer, and you're on your own.

Property managers aren't in the laundry business. Their job is the rental itself. Expecting laundry logistics from them is like expecting your hotel's front desk to do your grocery shopping.

Option 3: Skipping It and Buying More Clothes

This is more common than you'd think. Disney Springs has clothing stores. Target and Walmart are accessible from most vacation areas. It seems easier in the moment — just buy a few more shirts, worry about it at home.

The actual cost: $30–$60 in clothing you probably didn't need, plus the hassle of getting it home in an already-full bag. And you still come home with a mountain of dirty laundry.
