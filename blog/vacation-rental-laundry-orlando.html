Vacation Rental Laundry in Orlando: What Airbnb & VRBO Hosts Need to Know

April 10, 2026 · 7 min read · By A7 Laundry

Orlando is one of the most competitive vacation rental markets in the country. With thousands of Airbnb and VRBO properties near Disney World, Universal, and Kissimmee resort communities — the difference between a 4-star and 5-star review often comes down to one thing: how clean and fresh the linens are.

And yet, laundry is consistently one of the most time-consuming and underestimated parts of running a vacation rental. This guide walks through what Orlando hosts need to know about managing linen turnover efficiently.

The Laundry Problem Every Orlando Host Faces

Most vacation rental hosts in Orlando manage laundry one of two ways:

  • In-unit washer/dryer — Cleaners wash linens on-site between guests. Slow, labor-intensive, and scales poorly when you have same-day turnovers.
  • Extra linen sets — You keep 2–3 sets per bed, cycle them out, and deal with the pile later. Requires storage, capital, and still needs washing eventually.

Both approaches work — but as your property books more nights, they create a bottleneck. Same-day turnovers become stressful. Late checkout/early checkin windows shrink your cleaning window to under 4 hours. Cleaners rush.

💡 What professional hosts do differently

High-occupancy Orlando hosts delegate laundry entirely to a pickup & delivery service. Cleaners strip the beds, bag everything, and the laundry is picked up while cleaning happens. Fresh linens arrive before or at the same time as the next guests.

What Items Need Washing Between Every Guest

This is non-negotiable for 5-star reviews and guest health. Every guest turnover should include:

  • All bed sheets and pillowcases (every set, every bed)
  • Bath towels, hand towels, washcloths
  • Pool towels (if provided)
  • Kitchen towels and dishcloths
  • Bathroom rugs (weekly minimum, or as soiled)
  • Decorative pillow covers (spot-check, wash every 2–4 guests)

What Can Go Every 2–4 Guests?

  • Comforters and duvet inserts (if duvet covers are used)
  • Mattress protectors
  • Bed skirts and decorative shams
  • Throw blankets in common areas

What About Comforter Cleaning for Vacation Rentals?

Comforters are the most-neglected item in most vacation rental laundry routines. Many hosts only wash comforters when there's a visible stain — but guests notice odors and stiffness even before visible dirt appears.

We recommend washing vacation rental comforters:

  • Every 3–4 guest stays if no duvet cover is used
  • Every 8–12 guest stays if a duvet cover is always used
  • Immediately after any pet stay, illness, or visible staining

How to Brief Your Cleaning Team

If you're setting up a laundry service for the first time, communicate clearly with your cleaning team:

  • Designate which bags are "laundry out" vs "laundry in" (use different colored bags or labels)
  • Set a pickup location (front door, garage, etc.)
  • Create a linen inventory checklist so nothing gets lost
  • Communicate delivery windows to your cleaner so they can plan around them
